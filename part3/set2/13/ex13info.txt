Exercise 13: Learn to use back_inserter with a home-made class

We keep things simple and avoid defining even iterators for our class, as,
even though that could be useful here especially when also using the copy
algorithm to output our class's contents, we strictly need to use
std::back_inserter to *fill* it only.

As such we can get away with defining the needed valut_type for the copy
algorithm, and writing out push_back that will be used by back_inserter.

In order to properly read lines from a stream in full, rather than by
whitespace, we make a small class that inherits publicly from string, and give
it its own extraction operator, which uses getline to read lines in full.
