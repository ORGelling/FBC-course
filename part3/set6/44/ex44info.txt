Exercise 44: define a scanner that may change its line number count

We add a lineNr() member to the scanner, and proceed as before. Our main
function makes as much use of the scanner's own functionality as possible,
storing the scanned contents in a string and outputting them when a full line
has been found. We note that at this point the scanner's d_line data member is
already acting in the next line's line number, so we have to decrement it in
the cout statement.

We make use of the regular expression matching to swallow the \n after any new
line number indicator, such that we do not have to use more complicated logic
to keep track of the correct structure of the lines and their numbered update
commands.
